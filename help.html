How Parents Can Help

Parents and media are among the most important factors influencing adolescent’s body image. With that in mind, below are some of the ways parents can help promote positive body image in adolescents. There are also many reliable websites providing useful parenting resources that parents can utilize on their journey to promote healthy body image to their children, such as the raisingchildren.net.au .

Talk to your child about bodies and body image
Discuss with your child about what is a “normal” and healthy body vs. what is not, and that the social body “ideals” are not the normative look for everyone. Studies suggest that enhancing parent-child communication surrounding this topic might contribute to a healthier weight and increased body satisfaction. In contrast, strained or awkward parent-child communication, especially with fathers, is associated with body dissatisfaction among both male and female adolescents. Communication is key!
Teach your child to be media literate
Adolescents nowadays live in a media-saturated world, and there are no limits to what kind of messages adolescents might be exposed to on daily basis. With all kinds of information at hand, adolescents must be adequately equipped with media literacy skills, such as the ability to critically access and evaluate media content. Among the things, parents can discuss with their child include the unhealthy body images that are perpetuated in the media, as well as the marketing strategy that may target an audience with body dissatisfaction. Indeed, evidence suggests that media literacy can be a protective factor against the negative effects of social media on adolescents’ body image.
Be a supportive parent!
Beyond communication, the overall parent-child relationship also influences adolescents’ body image. In general, being in a secure parent-child relationship make adolescents less likely to conform to society’s beauty standard and is also linked to a healthier body image. A supportive mother-child relationship also helps buffer the negative effects of social media on teen body satisfaction.
Promote healthy habits
This includes encouraging healthy eating and physical activities, as well as limiting screen time. It is important to set a good example through your own behavior and be a positive role model to your child. After all, they do say that healthy habits start at home!
Watch out for signs of body image concerns
If your child is showing signs of body dissatisfaction such as criticizing their body, frequently comparing their body with others, and obsessing about losing weight or increasing muscles, go back to step 1! Talk to them and identify the root of the problem. Be sure to stay supportive as you guide them through it.
