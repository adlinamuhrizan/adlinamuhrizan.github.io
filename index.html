Overview

A person’s body image is how they think and feel about their body. This may include what they believe about their appearance in their mind, which may or may not reflect their true shape and size. In general, one can have a positive body image, meaning that they are satisfied with their body, or negative body image, meaning they are unhappy with the way they look. Having a positive body image is a rather important part of mental and physical well-being, whereas having a negative body image is associated with many negative consequences including depression and disordered eating.

The prevalence of body image dissatisfaction among adolescents is particularly concerning: Estimates vary by different studies due to sampling difference, for example, 60.4% among Brazilian adolescents and 36.7% among adolescents in UAE, but the statistics, nonetheless, show that body dissatisfaction is, indeed, of high prevalence among adolescence worldwide.

Sex Differences on Body Image Concerns

When considering the big picture, body dissatisfaction is more prevalent among girls than boys, especially in early adolescence. However, this does not mean that boys do not experience body dissatisfaction – they do have body image concerns as well, though there are important sex differences in body dissatisfaction among boys than girls.

Female

  • Body dissatisfaction is primarily associated with the desire to become thinner among girls
  • More than 70% of high school girls sample reported the desire to be thinner
  • More likely than boys to adopt strategies to lose weight, e.g., dieting to address body image concerns

Male

  • Being heavy or thin are both sources of body dissatisfaction for boys
  • Those who are overweight want to be thinner, and those who were underweight want to be larger
  • More likely than girls to address body image concerns by adopting muscle increase strategies, e.g., exercising
